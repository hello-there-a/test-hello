More than 26,000 children are forced to beg every day to feed their families

Why children are forced to beg

Parents forcing children to work

Many parents strive to provide a fulfilling life for their children, while some unfortunately exploit their children's labor. Such practices, which violate legal standards regarding labor rights, can lead to severe legal consequences for those involved.

Children's family is in poverty

Certain children are compelled to engage in begging or labor due to their families' financial struggles. Despite all family members working, poverty persists, leading to the unfortunate necessity for children to contribute to the family's income.

Family in debt

Many families face significant financial challenges, often resulting in overwhelming debt. In such circumstances, it becomes essential for all household members to seek employment opportunities to alleviate their financial obligations.

Problems with children begging

Children who are forced to beg are constantly interacting with new strangers, because of that, the children are constantly under threat of kidnappings, assault, and worse.

The struggle to exit suffering

Children, even though they are trying their best to make money, they have a very little chance of actually accomplishing their goal of making enough money to escape poverty.
